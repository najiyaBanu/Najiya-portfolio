Writing Space

Hope in Cage

The cage we call a city's bone,
Each corner wired, each child alone.
Eyes far away watch half in grief,
Some sip their tea; some praise the thief.

Bread turned weapon, hope turned bait,
they carry silence, bearing its weight.
Across the sea, small boats appear,
With bags of bread and voices clear.

They sail through dark to break the chain,
To feed the pigeons lost in pain.
But iron gates and guns awake,
they steal the oars, the sails they break.

hands that reach get bound in night,
The pigeons wait beyond their sight.
Old shadows whisper through the flame —
Another holocaust, same old name.

— N.B.

A Piece of Solitude

The quiet center of my heart adores a thing
which leads gently into solitude —
a path from which my heart can never return.

Where I am no longer myself,
and my soul is no longer mine.

— N.B.
